How MatchForge scores

Every number in your shortlist is computed the same way, every time, from published weights — no hidden adjustments. This page is the complete methodology.

1 · Fit — how well they match you

Grok reads the profile and returns three sub-scores for your goals: compatibility, attractiveness, and red flags (0–100 each). Your fit score is then computed deterministically from your own weights — the model never decides the overall number:

fit = compatibility × wc + attractiveness × wa + (100 − red flags) × wr

Default weights are 40% / 30% / 30%. Trust signals are deliberately not shown to the model here — fit measures fit, nothing else.

2 · Trust — five dimensions, one score

Each profile is vetted across up to five dimensions. The overall trust score is their weighted average; if a dimension couldn't be analyzed, its weight is redistributed over the others — a missing check lowers confidence, never the score itself.

DimensionWeightWhat it detects
Authenticity35%Real person vs AI-generated or stolen photos
Catfish risk30%Photo inconsistencies, known scam patterns
Naturalness15%Heavy filters and beauty editing
Bot risk10%Template bios, spam and automation patterns
Consistency10%Photo-to-photo and photo-to-bio coherence
𝕏 Social proof15%*Account age, follower health, claim checks via the X API + Grok's live investigation

*Added to the blend only after you run an X verification; all weights renormalize.

3 · Match — trust applied exactly once

Your match score is fit with a single, documented trust penalty. Risk below 30 is treated as noise; above that, the penalty grows linearly:

match = fit × (1 − max(0, risk − 30) ÷ 140)  where risk = max(catfish, bot)

If catfish risk reaches 70+, the match score is capped at 35 outright — a profile that's probably fake can't rank well no matter how attractive it looks. Every card's “How this score was computed” panel shows this arithmetic: fit − penalty = match.

4 · List order

The shortlist sorts by match score. Only two things can move a profile away from that order, and both are labeled on the card: your own feedback (Top / Like / Pass) and an X verification result.

5 · Confidence

Every trust score carries a confidence tier — high, medium, or low — based on how much evidence backed it: photos analyzed, bio signals, public web footprint, and X verification. Hover the tier to see exactly what it was based on.

6 · When a check can't run

If a vision or analysis call fails, that dimension is shown as “not analyzed” — grey, no number. We never substitute made-up neutral scores, and absence of evidence is never treated as risk: a person with no public footprint is marked unverified, not suspicious.

Honest limits

These scores are probabilistic decision-support from AI models — they are not a background check and not ground truth about any person. Filters get misjudged, real people get flagged, fakes slip through. Use the scores to prioritize your own verification, and always confirm a match is real before meeting anyone.
